Peter Dinklage is a well-known American actor, best known for playing Tyrion Lannister on HBO’s “Game of Thrones.” His salary for each Game of Thrones episode was $1.1 million, making him one of the highest-paid TV actors ever.

Besides Game of Thrones, he has acted in movies such as “Elf,” “Cyrano,” “Pixels,” and “Avengers: Infinity War.” However, fans are curious to know how much Peter Dinklage has earned throughout his career. In this post, you’ll find all the information related to his earnings.

How Much is Peter Dinklage Net Worth?

As of 2023, Peter Dinklage’s net worth is estimated to be $25 million, a testament to his successful career in the film and television industry.

How Much Did Peter Dinklage Earn from ‘Game of Thrones’?”

Peter Dinklage received a salary episode of $1.1 million for the 14 episodes that aired in the final two seasons of Game of Thrones, which ran from July 2017 to May 2019. Throughout those two seasons, that comes to $14.3 million.

At this point, that episode salary ranks as the sixth highest any performer has ever received from a television program. He received $10 million in total compensation for the 20 episodes he shot for seasons 5 and 6. Each episode cost $500,000. Peter probably makes more than $30 million in total from Game of Thrones.

How Did Peter Dinklage Become Famous?

Dinklage’s passion for acting began at an early age when he and his brothers performed puppet plays for the locals in their New Jersey neighborhood. In his musical performance of The Velveteen Rabbit in fifth grade, he played his first lead part of any kind.

However, he landed his first paid role in 1995, appearing in Steve Buscemi’s independent meta-film Living in Oblivion. Filmed in New York City on a tight budget, Dinklage played an actor who is fed up with playing cliched roles. Thus, the part was a reflection of his challenges growing up as a dwarf actor in many ways.

Early in his career, Dinklage swore he would turn down parts that humiliated or stereotyped him. In light of this, he devoted the majority of the 1990s and 2000s to taking on difficult roles in indie dramas and comedies, such as 2003’s The Station Agent.

He appeared in direct-to-video productions (TipToes), low-budget films (Find Me Guilty, The Baxter), and theater (Richard III). He began work on a project that helped elevate his profile in Hollywood in 2008 when he was cast in Prince Caspian, the second part of C.S. Lewis’s The Chronicles of Narnia.

Peter Dinklage Real Estate Investment

After Game of Thrones, Peter Dinklage got a fancy house near New York City. It has 8 bedrooms, outdoor and indoor pools, a gaming room, and a bowling alley, as per Forbes.

Because he likes privacy, he added a home theater to watch movies without media disturbance. The house cost $15 million, around 19% of Dinklage’s net worth.
