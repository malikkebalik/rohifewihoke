Mama Cax Cancer And Death

Well, she didn’t exactly die of Cancer! However, her lung Cancer is heavily linked to the medical complications that killed her on December 16, 2019.

The birth name of Mama Cax, a model and activist, was Cacsmy Brutus, and she was born in Brooklyn, New York, on November 20, 1989. She was raised in Port-au-Prince, Haiti.

Mama Cax was diagnosed with bone and lung Cancer at the young age of 14. Due to her Cancer, Mama Cax underwent hip replacement surgery at 16 that was unsuccessful, resulting in the amputation of her right leg.

The Haitian American Model shared her health journey on Instagram a few days before her death. She uncovered blood clots in her leg, thigh, abdomen, and near a Cancer treatment filter in her lungs during a photoshoot in London.

Mama Cax’s family shared on Instagram that she lost her battle with her illness after a week of fighting in the Royal London Hospital on December 16. Her strength was the same as when she previously fought against Cancer.

Mama Cax: Suicide Rumors Of American-Haitian Model 

If you’ve stumbled upon any suicide rumors of the American-Haitian Model, we would like to tell you that those rumors are entirely fake. 

Although there were some medical complications, she was a strong woman who battled and survived severe Bone and Lung Cancer at 16. 

The Model and activist Mama Cax left a lasting legacy by expanding the image of people with disabilities and will be remembered for her efforts in this regard.

Despite the numerous difficulties she faced in her early life, Mama Cax emerged with a strengthened sense of self, blazed a trail for amputees, and soared to prominence, always on her terms.

Her love for fashion and activism became a successful career, including talks at the White House and Global Forum. Mama Cax revitalized others by sharing her life experiences on Instagram.

Tribute: Mama Cax In The Modelling Industry

On September 15, 2016, she was part of a White House fashion show hosted by the Obamas. Balancing both, she worked in the NYC Mayor’s office while studying.

At 18, Cax picked up the sport of wheelchair basketball and held a Bachelor’s and Master’s degree in International Relations.

Cax entered the modelling industry through a commercial advertisement in 2017, leading to her signing with JAG Models, located in New York.

Her modelling portfolio includes runway shows for Chromat and Fenty Beauty and advertisements for Tommy Hilfiger and Sephora.

During New York Fashion Week in 2018, Cax showcased a swimsuit designed by Becca McCharen, who aims to challenge conventional beauty norms.

In the same year, Cax was featured on the cover of Teen Vogue along with Jillian Mercado and Chelsea Werner.

Cax became the spokesperson for Olay’s Sunscreen Campaign in 2019 and later announced her plan to compete in the New York Marathon with a wheelchair.

Although the American-Haitian Model and Activist died early at 30 on December 16, 2019, her legacy and modelling career will never be forgotten.
