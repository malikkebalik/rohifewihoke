When did Kavan Smith Make His Acting Debut?

Smith was raised by his father after his parents divorced in Edmonton, Alberta, where he was born. He spent a year and a half at the University of Calgary before enrolling in Mount Royal University’s performing arts department.

In 1993, he made his acting debut in the starring role of Destiny Ridge. Smith played the recurring role of Major Evan Lorne in 29 episodes of Stargate: Atlantis. Additionally, he played Major Lorne in two episodes of Stargate SG-1, “Enemy Mine” in Season 7 and “The Road Not Taken” in Season 10.

Additionally, he has appeared in a number of television programs as a guest star including The Twilight Zone, Smallville, Sanctuary, Tru Calling, Battlestar Galactica and Outer Limits. On Syfy’s Eureka, he played the android Deputy Andy in recurrent episodes.

In the 2012 SyFy movie Red: Werewolf Hunter which depicts the contemporary, grim Red Riding Hood tale, Smith co-starred with Felicia Day. In 2010, he also played the lead in the SyFy film Metal Shifters. Kavan has voiced the Canadian documentaries Combat School and Jetstream in addition to acting.
